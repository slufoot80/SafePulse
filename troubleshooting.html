Setup & Troubleshooting

Welcome! This guide walks you through installing and configuring SafePulse so it can actually save your life. Takes about 5 minutes.

What SafePulse does

SafePulse asks you to "check in" on a schedule you choose. If you don't check in by the deadline, it automatically sends an emergency SMS to people you trust (and optionally calls them). You can also press the red SOS button at any time to send the alert immediately.

After installing

  1. Tap Get Started.
  2. Select 30 minutes for initial testing — you can change this later in Settings.
  3. Tap Allow Access to give SafePulse access to your contacts.
  4. Pick the contacts you want to alert (up to 10).
  5. On the alert methods step, choose SMS Contact, and Make Call if you want the app to also phone the first person on your list.
  6. Tap the Settings menu (top-right, next to the 3 dots) and turn on:
    • SMS — sends the emergency text automatically.
    • Auto-Call — automatically dials your first contact.
    • When prompted with "Phone permission needed," tap Allow.
  7. Go back into Contacts and tap the green check mark next to each person to approve them. Only approved contacts get alerts.

Approve your contacts — this is critical

Adding a contact is not enough. Each person must also be approved before SafePulse will send them an alert.

Open the Contacts screen and tap the green ✔ next to each person. Only approved contacts will receive emergency alerts.

Critical permissions — do not skip

SafePulse needs several Android permissions to work. If you skip any of these, the app may fail silently when you need it most. Grant them all when prompted during onboarding, then manually verify every one under Phone Settings → Apps → SafePulse → Permissions.

On newer Android versions (12 and up), several of these permissions are denied by default even though the app requests them — you must toggle them on yourself.

Permission Why it’s needed Where to enable
SMS To send the emergency text without you needing to tap Send. Settings → Apps → SafePulse → Permissions → SMS → Allow
Contacts To pick emergency contacts from your address book. Settings → Apps → SafePulse → Permissions → Contacts → Allow
Location (Precise) To include your GPS location in the emergency SMS. Settings → Apps → SafePulse → Permissions → Location → Allow all the time (or at minimum “Allow only while using the app”), and choose Precise.
Notifications To remind you before your check-in deadline. Settings → Apps → SafePulse → Notifications → Allow
Alarms & reminders ⚠ Critical — without this, the app cannot fire the missed-check-in alert at the exact deadline. Settings → Apps → SafePulse → Permissions → Additional permissions → Alarms & reminders (or Settings → Apps → Special app access → Alarms & reminders).
Full screen intent ⚠ Required for the 5-minute warning and emergency alert to break through your lock screen. Settings → Apps → SafePulse → Appear on top / Display over other apps, OR Settings → Apps → SafePulse → Full screen intent — varies by phone.
Battery — Unrestricted ⚠ Without this, Android will kill the app after reboot or during idle and skip the alert entirely. Settings → Apps → SafePulse → Battery → Unrestricted (NOT “Optimized”).

Samsung, Xiaomi, OnePlus, Huawei, Oppo, Vivo users

Your phone has an extra layer of aggressive battery management on top of standard Android. You must also:

  • Add SafePulse to the “Never sleeping apps” / “Auto-launch” list. Samsung: Settings → Device care → Battery → Background usage limits → Never sleeping apps.
  • Disable any “Adaptive battery” learning for SafePulse.
  • If your phone has “Protected apps” (Huawei) or “Autostart management” (Xiaomi/Oppo/Vivo), enable it for SafePulse.

Set your name

In Settings, tap the profile row at the top and enter your name. Your name appears in the SMS so contacts know who needs help.

Test it before you rely on it

Don’t wait until a real emergency to find out something isn’t configured right.

  1. Make sure at least one emergency contact is added and approved (green ✔).
  2. Tell that contact: “I’m about to send you a test SafePulse alert — please confirm you got it.”
  3. Tap the red SOS button on the main screen and confirm.
  4. Within a few seconds you should see:
    • A small message at the bottom of the screen saying “Sending SOS to 1 contact…”
    • Your contact receives an SMS with the SOS message and your location link.
  5. Ask your contact to confirm they received it.

If nothing visible happened when you pressed SOS, something is misconfigured — check the Common Issues section below.

What your contacts will see

When you miss a check-in, your approved contacts get a text like this:

🚨 SAFEPULSE EMERGENCY ALERT 🚨 [Your Name] has NOT checked in and may need help. Last location: https://maps.google.com/?q=42.5,-83.2 ⚠ CALL THEM NOW. If no answer, call 911.

When you press SOS:

🚨 SOS — SAFEPULSE EMERGENCY 🚨 [Your Name] triggered an SOS and needs IMMEDIATE HELP. Location: https://maps.google.com/?q=42.5,-83.2 ⚠ CALL [Your Name] NOW. If unreachable, call 911.

Tell your contacts to expect this

Before relying on the app, tell each of your emergency contacts:

  1. You added them to a safety check-in app called SafePulse.
  2. They’ll get an automated text if you don’t respond on time, or if you press SOS.
  3. Save your number to their contacts so the alert shows your name (not “Unknown”).
  4. Add you as a Priority contact in their phone (or allow your messages through Do Not Disturb) so the alert isn’t muted at night.
  5. Always treat the alert as real — call you immediately, then 911 if you don’t pick up.

Common issues

“No approved contacts” message appears when pressing SOS

Open Contacts and tap the green ✔ next to each one. Only approved contacts get alerts.

Pressed SOS and nothing visible happened

The SMS permission is almost always the cause. Go to Settings → Apps → SafePulse → Permissions → SMS and set to Allow. Also confirm the in-app SMS toggle is ON (open the app’s Settings screen).

SMS doesn’t arrive at your contact

Check that the recipient’s phone number includes country code (the app should normalize this automatically). Check your SMS permission. Look for a small message at the bottom of the screen (“SMS sent to…” or an error code) right after pressing SOS.

Missed-check-in alert never fires

The Alarms & reminders permission is missing. Grant it under Settings → Apps → SafePulse → Permissions → Additional permissions.

Alarm didn’t fire after I rebooted my phone

Two common causes on Android 12+: (1) battery optimization killed the app after reboot — set Battery to Unrestricted; (2) on Samsung/Xiaomi/OnePlus/Huawei/Oppo/Vivo, the phone blocked the app from auto-starting — add SafePulse to your phone’s “Never sleeping apps” / “Auto-launch” / “Protected apps” list. After fixing, reboot once more and verify by pressing SOS.

5-min warning / emergency alert doesn’t pop up over the lock screen

Grant the Full screen intent permission (Settings → Apps → SafePulse → Full screen intent, or on some phones “Display over other apps”).

Location doesn’t appear in the SMS

Toggle “Include Location” off and back on in Settings. When the dialog appears, choose Allow precise location.

App keeps showing “Get Started”

Walk through to the end and tap Enable & Start on the last step.

Privacy

SafePulse stores everything on your device only. Nothing is sent to a server. The only network traffic the app generates is the SMS itself, sent through your phone’s normal carrier connection.
